# HR-Analytics-Dashboard
## 📌 Overview
A two-page Power BI dashboard analyzing employee records — covering gender distribution, salary insights, departmental composition, attrition trends, and payroll data.

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Enable HR teams to drill down into employee demographics, compensation, and turnover patterns by department, hire year, and job title for better workforce planning.

## 🗂️ Data Source
Employee records dataset, queried and structured using MySQL

## 🛠️ Tools Used
Power BI, DAX, Power Query, MySQL

## 🔍 Key Analysis
**Page 1 — HR Analysis Overview**
- Gender distribution and headcount across 300K total employees
- Employees by hire year and department
- Employee resignation count by department
- Department-wise employee share and average salaries
- Average salary trend by year (1985–2000)
- Salary distribution by job title and department

**Page 2 — Turnover & Payroll Detail**
- Department with the highest turnover (waterfall view), led by Sales at 23.44%
- Total employees, average salary, and total payroll breakdown
- Interactive filters by hired date range, department, and title

## 💡 Key Insights
- Workforce of 300K employees, with an average salary of 63.81K and a near-even gender split (59.99% male, 40.01% female)
- Development is the largest department at 25.65% of total headcount, followed by Production (22.22%) and Sales (15.64%)
- Sales carries the highest average department salary at 81K, while Human Resources is lowest at 56K
- Sales also recorded the highest resignation count (10,997) and highest cumulative turnover contribution (23.44%) among departments
- Average salary has grown steadily from 53K (1985) to 73K (2000), reflecting consistent year-over-year compensation growth
